FOUNDATION

A sensible investing path

Beginner
01

Protect the downside

Clear expensive debt, keep an emergency fund, and buy adequate health and term insurance before investing aggressively.

02

Define the goal

Choose a time horizon and acceptable loss. Short-term goals belong in lower-risk instruments, not volatile equities.

03

Start diversified

For many beginners, a low-cost broad-market index fund is simpler and less concentrated than selecting individual shares.

04

Research businesses

Study revenue, profit, cash flow, debt, valuation, management quality, risks, and exchange filings before buying a company.

05

Size positions

Avoid putting most of your portfolio into one stock, sector, tip, or theme. Add gradually rather than chasing sudden moves.

06

Review, do not react

Review goals and allocation periodically. A price fall alone is not a reason to sell; a broken investment thesis may be.

TECHNICAL BASICS

Read patterns as probabilities

Patterns can fail. Confirm them with volume, trend, support/resistance, business news, and risk controls.

Intermediate

Uptrend

Higher highs and higher lows suggest buyers remain in control. Avoid buying only because price has already risen sharply.

Watch: pullbacks holding above prior support

Support & resistance

Support is an area where demand previously appeared; resistance is where selling previously appeared. They are zones, not exact prices.

Watch: price reaction and volume near the zone

Breakout

Price moves above a well-tested resistance zone. False breakouts are common when volume is weak or the broader market is falling.

Watch: close above resistance with stronger volume

Head and shoulders

A possible reversal structure with a higher middle peak. It is not confirmed until price breaks the neckline.

Watch: neckline break and failed recovery

Moving averages

Moving averages smooth price trends. They lag price and should not be treated as automatic buy or sell instructions.

Watch: trend direction and repeated price reactions

Downtrend

Lower highs and lower lows suggest sellers remain in control. A cheap-looking price can keep becoming cheaper.

Watch: reversal confirmation before entering

HIGH-RISK PRODUCTS

Trading and F&O

Advanced only
Most individual F&O traders lose money.

SEBI reported that 91% of individual equity-derivatives traders incurred net losses in FY 2024–25. Leverage can create losses much larger and faster than expected.

Futures

A leveraged obligation to buy or sell later. Daily mark-to-market losses can trigger margin calls. Loss potential can be substantial.

Options buying

Loss is generally limited to premium paid, but time decay and volatility changes can make options lose value even when direction seems correct.

Options selling

Premium received is limited while losses can be very large. Margin availability is not a measure of affordability or safety.

Risk rules

Use paper trading first, define maximum loss before entry, avoid averaging leveraged losses, and never trade to recover a previous loss.
